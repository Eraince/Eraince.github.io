Jobs at Toastmasters

An important element of Toastmasters is everyone partipates. In every meeting, every one has a role. Either actively as a speaker or evaluator or the Toastmaster of the Day, or as attentive listeners who give feedback.

  • Toastmaster of The Day

    The Toastmaster is a meeting’s director and host. As Toastmaster of the day, one is responsible for ensuring all of the meeting’s players know their parts and hit their marks.

  • General Evaluator

    Ensuring the speech and leadership project evaluators know their responsibilities. Evaluating everything that takes place during the club meeting. Making sure each activity is performed correctly.

  • Table Topic Chair

  • Speakers

  • Speech Evaluators

    The evaluator of each speech will provide both verbal and written evaluations to the speaker using the guide in the manual.Making sure each activity is performed correctly.

  • Grammarian

    One benefit of Toastmasters is that it helps people improve their grammar and word use. It is responsibility of the grammarian to point that out. Also, it provides an exercise in expanding listening skills.

  • Ah-Um Counter

    The purpose of the Ah-Um Counter is to note any word or sound used as a crutch by anyone who speaks during the meeting. Words may be inappropriate interjections, such as and, well, but, so and you know.

  • Self-Presentation Monitor

  • Time-Keeper and Vote-Counter

  • Thought of the Day

    A thought or an inspiring quotation that sets the tone for the day’s meeting.
